General Info.

CMCA stands for Chinese Music and Culture Association, a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization.

Our mission is to build a platform for community, artistic expression, and cultural enrichment through choral and a cappella music, education, and cultural exchange.

We offer performance opportunities, community arts events, workshops and masterclasses, youth arts programs, and support for emerging arts groups. We do not sell or promote restricted goods or services.

About our programs

Find A Cappella is a semi‑professional a cappella singing group, and the first project of CMCA.

We are a group of vocal singers, music lovers, and young professionals based in the Bay Area. We’ve presented annual concerts with diverse repertoire—from pop and musical theater to jazz, folk, and sacred music. We aim to deliver good vibes to our audience and the community.

Sudo Chorus (also referred to as Sudo Choir) is formed by passionate amateur and semi‑professional singers. The group rehearses weekly and performs multiple times a year across a wide range of repertoire.

Recent highlights include the 2025 New Year's Concert: Echoes of the World on January 18–19, 2025, celebrating global harmony and cultural expression through choral music.

Mandarin A Cappella Workshop is a welcoming, drop‑in program for anyone interested in singing Mandarin‑language a cappella, regardless of experience.

Sessions feature original arrangements by our members, cover a variety of styles, and focus on vocal technique, blend, and harmony—no instruments required.

Láirén Improv, founded in 2023, is the Bay Area's first Chinese-language improv comedy group.

We offer monthly improv shows and workshops, creating unscripted, one-of-a-kind performances. Nobody really dislikes improv — they just haven't tried it yet! We're on a mission to help more people discover improv, discover Chinese improv, and discover Láirén Improv!
